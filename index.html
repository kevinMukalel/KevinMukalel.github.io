Mechatronics Engineering

I like to think of myself as a very motivated indvidual with a passion for learning new skills. Over the last year, as an Engineering student at the University of Waterloo, I have had the opportunity to meet highly talented individuals and put my skills to the test in various ways. In my first year, my interest and willingness to learn about cars and cleaner energy lead me to the Formula Electric Design team. As a result, I was challenged to both contribute to the team in addition to maintaining my academics.

Through my first co-op term I was able to largely expand my reservoir of skills by working for three different start-up companies. My experience ranges from Fullstack web development and UX design to 3D modelling, drafting and machining. I plan to continue developing these skills through various co-op opportunities and become a well-rounded Mechatronics Engineer.

Work Expereience

For my first co-op term, I had the privilege of joining the Bridging Entrepreneurs to Students Program. Working at three different unique companies gave me a broad experience and the opportunity to learn various skills.

Projects

Here are some of the projects I've had the pleasure of working on.

Rubik's Cube Solving Robot

Worked with a team of four engineering students to design and build a robot capable of solving a Rubik's Cube in 26 minutes using C++, RobotC and Lego EV3 components.

Robotic Arm Challenge

Collaborated effectively with a team of four students under time sensitive conditions to construct a robotic arm that picks up plastic bones and relocates them to a targeted area using a combination of servo motors and a pneumatic cylinder.

Dynamometer Testing Motor Mount

Designed and fabricated a mounting plate to firmly secure the electric car motor during dynamometer testing. Other Formula E projects include rear tripod housing adjustments and the design of steel inserts.
